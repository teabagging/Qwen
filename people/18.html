❓Please explain the following problem in detail and provide code in Ruby:You are given two arrays of positive integers, `boxes` and `warehouse`, representing the heights of some boxes of unit width and the heights of `n` rooms in a warehouse respectively. The warehouse's rooms are labeled from `0` to `n - 1` from left to right where `warehouse[i]` (0-indexed) is the height of the `ith` room.Boxes are put into the warehouse by the following rules:* Boxes cannot be stacked.* You can rearrange the insertion order of the boxes.* Boxes can be pushed into the warehouse from **either side** (left or right)* If the height of some room in the warehouse is less than the height of a box, then that box and all other boxes behind it will be stopped before that room.Return _the maximum number of boxes you can put into the warehouse._**Example 1:****Input:** boxes = [1,2,2,3,4], warehouse = [3,4,1,2]**Output:** 4**Explanation:** We can store the boxes in the following order:1- Put the yellow box in room 2 from either the left or right side.2- Put the orange box in room 3 from the right side.3- Put the green box in room 1 from the left side.4- Put the red box in room 0 from the left side.Notice that there are other valid ways to put 4 boxes such as swapping the red and green boxes or the red and orange boxes.**Example 2:****Input:** boxes = [3,5,5,2], warehouse = [2,1,3,4,5]**Output:** 3**Explanation:** It is not possible to put the two boxes of height 5 in the warehouse since there's only 1 room of height >= 5.Other valid solutions are to put the green box in room 2 or to put the orange box first in room 2 before putting the green and red boxes.**Constraints:*** `n == warehouse.length`* `1 <= boxes.length, warehouse.length <= 105`* `1 <= boxes[i], warehouse[i] <= 109`

💡We are given two arrays, `boxes` and `warehouse`, representing heights of boxes and rooms in a warehouse. We need to find the maximum number of boxes we can put into the warehouse. We can rearrange the order of boxes and can push boxes from either the left or right end of the warehouse. If a room is shorter than a box, the box and any subsequent boxes being pushed from that direction are stopped. Boxes cannot be stacked, meaning each room can hold at most one box (in terms of the problem description, it's implied that we are trying to fit boxes into rooms sequentially from left to right or right to left until we are stopped by a too-short room). Let's first understand the constraint of "stopped before that room". If we are pushing from the left, and we are trying to put a box into room i, and if `warehouse[i]` is less than the box height, then this box and any further boxes we are trying to push from the left direction cannot be placed in rooms starting from room i. The same applies if we are pushing from the right, and we are trying to put a box into room i. If `warehouse[i]` is shorter than the box, then this box and any subsequent boxes pushed from the right direction cannot be placed in rooms up to and including room i.Let's think about the warehouse. Since we can push from either left or right, it seems important to know the available heights of rooms as we move from left to right and right to left. Consider pushing boxes from the left. When we try to place a box into room 0, it can only be placed if the box's height is less than or equal to `warehouse[0]`. If it is, we successfully place it. Then we try to place the next box into room 1. Again, it can be placed if its height is less than or equal to `warehouse[1]`. And so on. However, the condition is about "stopped before that room". It's perhaps better to think about it as follows: when we are trying to place a box, say of height h. If we are trying to place it into room i, we must have h le warehouse[i]. If this condition is met, we can potentially place it there. If we're pushing from the left, and we've successfully placed boxes in rooms 0, 1, ..., i-1, and we are trying to place a box in room i. If we cannot place it in room i, then we cannot place this box, and also we cannot place any more boxes from the left into room i or any room to its right. A similar logic applies if we are pushing from the right.Let's consider an example. `boxes = [1, 2, 2, 3, 4]`, `warehouse = [3, 4, 1, 2]`. Warehouse room heights are [3, 4, 1, 2].
