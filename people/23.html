A bookstore is ordering books for the upcoming month. They already have 30 books on their shelves but they need a total of 150 books. Of the books they still need to order, they decide to buy a third from a new publisher, and then they go to their regular supplier for the remaining books. How many books will the bookstore order from the regular supplier?

Let's break this problem down step-by-step.1. **Understand the Goal:** The problem asks for the number of books ordered from the regular supplier.2. **Identify Known Information:** * Current books on shelves: 30 * Total books needed: 150 * Fraction of books from new publisher: one-third (1/3) of the books they *still need to order*. * Remaining books from regular supplier.3. **Determine the Unknown:** * Number of books to order in total. * Number of books ordered from the new publisher. * Number of books ordered from the regular supplier.4. **Plan the Steps to Solve:** * Step 1: Calculate the number of books they still need to order. This is the difference between the total books needed and the books they already have. * Step 2: Calculate the number of books ordered from the new publisher. This is one-third of the books they still need to order (calculated in step 1). * Step 3: Calculate the number of books ordered from the regular supplier. This is the remaining books after ordering from the new publisher from the books they still need to order (calculated in step 1).5. **Execute the Plan (Step-by-step calculations):** * Step 1: Books still needed = Total books needed - Current books on shelves Books still needed = 150 - 30 = 120 books * Step 2: Books from new publisher = (1/3) * Books still needed Books from new publisher = (1/3) * 120 = 120 / 3 = 40 books * Step 3: Books from regular supplier = Books still needed - Books from new publisher Books from regular supplier = 120 - 40 = 80 books6. **Verify the Answer:** * Total books ordered = Books from new publisher + Books from regular supplier = 40 + 80 = 120 * Total books after ordering = Current books on shelves + Total books ordered = 30 + 120 = 150 * This matches the total books needed, so the calculations seem correct. * The problem asks for the number of books from the regular supplier, which is calculated to be 80.7. **State the Final Answer:** The bookstore will order 80 books from the regular supplier.Double check understanding:The phrase "Of the books they still need to order, they decide to buy a third from a new publisher" is key. It is a third of the *needed* books, not a third of the total books required, or a third of the books from the regular supplier. My interpretation and steps are consistent with this.

Sand is poured into a box so that the box is being filled at the rate of 4 cubic feet per hour. The empty rectangular box has a certain length, is 6 feet wide, and 2 feet deep. It takes approximately 21 hours to fill the box. What is the length of the box in feet?

Let V be the volume of the rectangular box.Let R be the rate at which sand is poured into the box, which is given as 4 cubic feet per hour.Let T be the time it takes to fill the box, which is given as 21 hours.The total volume of sand poured into the box is the rate of pouring multiplied by the time it takes to fill the box.V = R * TV = 4 cubic feet/hour * 21 hoursV = 4 * 21 cubic feetV = 84 cubic feetThe box is rectangular, so the volume of a rectangular box is given by the formula:V = length * width * depthWe are given the following dimensions of the box:Width = 6 feetDepth = 2 feetLet the length of the box be L feet.So, the volume of the box in terms of its dimensions is:V = L * 6 feet * 2 feetV = 12L cubic feetWe know that the volume of the box is 84 cubic feet (from the rate and time calculation).So, we can set the two expressions for volume equal to each other:12L = 84To find the length L, we need to solve for L in the equation 12L = 84.Divide both sides of the equation by 12:L = 84 / 12To simplify 84 / 12, we can divide both numbers by their common factors.Both 84 and 12 are divisible by 12.84 / 12 = 712 / 12 = 1So, L = 7 feet.Let's double check the calculations.Volume = Length * Width * Depth = 7 feet * 6 feet * 2 feet = 7 * 12 cubic feet = 84 cubic feet.Rate of filling = 4 cubic feet per hour.Time to fill = Volume / Rate = 84 cubic feet / (4 cubic feet per hour) = 84 / 4 hours.84 / 4 = (80 + 4) / 4 = 80/4 + 4/4 = 20 + 1 = 21 hours.This matches the given time to fill the box.So, the length of the box is 7 feet.

Let F be the number of female members and M be the number of male members.Let A_F be the average age of female members, A_M be the average age of male members, and A_T be the average age of the entire membership.We are given:A_F = 40 years oldA_M = 25 years oldA_T = 30 years oldWe want to find the ratio of the female to male members, which is F/M.The sum of the ages of all female members is F times A_F = 40F.The sum of the ages of all male members is M times A_M = 25M.The sum of the ages of all members is the sum of ages of female members plus the sum of ages of male members, which is 40F + 25M.The total number of members is F + M.The average age of the entire membership is the total sum of ages divided by the total number of members.A_T = frac{40F + 25M}{F + M}We are given that A_T = 30.So, 30 = frac{40F + 25M}{F + M}To solve for the ratio F/M, we can multiply both sides by (F + M):30(F + M) = 40F + 25M30F + 30M = 40F + 25MNow, we want to isolate the terms with F on one side and terms with M on the other side.Subtract 30F from both sides:30M = 40F - 30F + 25M30M = 10F + 25MSubtract 25M from both sides:30M - 25M = 10F5M = 10FNow we want to find the ratio F/M. Divide both sides by 10M:frac{5M}{10M} = frac{10F}{10M}frac{5}{10} = frac{F}{M}frac{1}{2} = frac{F}{M}So the ratio of female to male members is 1/2.To verify, let's assume there are F=1 female member and M=2 male members.The sum of ages of female members is 1 times 40 = 40.The sum of ages of male members is 2 times 25 = 50.The total sum of ages is 40 + 50 = 90.The total number of members is 1 + 2 = 3.The average age of the entire membership is 90 / 3 = 30.This matches the given average age of the entire membership.The ratio of female to male members is F/M = 1/2.The question asked for the answer as a common fraction.The fraction is 1/2.
