What defines a valid IPv4 address? * Four numerical parts, separated by periods (`.`). * Each part (octet) must be a number between 0 and 255 (inclusive).2. **Initial Approach - Splitting and Validation:** The most straightforward approach is to: * Split the input string by the period (`.`) delimiter. * Check if we get exactly four parts after splitting. If not, it's not a valid IPv4 address. * For each part: * Try to convert it to an integer. If this fails (e.g., it's not a number), it's invalid. * Check if the integer is within the valid range (0-255). frac{k}{2} = 2.5-k - frac{k}{2} = 25-2 = k + frac{k}{2}3 = frac{2k+k}{2} = frac{3k}{2}3 = frac{3k}{2}Multiply both sides by frac{2}{3}:3 cdot frac{2}{3} = frac{3k}{2} cdot frac{2}{3}2 = kSo, when k=2, the term will have x^2.Now we need to find the coefficient of the term with k=2.For k=2, the term is T_{2+1} = T_3 = binom{5}{2} (2x)^{5-2} (-frac{1}{sqrt{x}})^2 = binom{5}{2} (2x)^3 (-frac{1}{sqrt{x}})^2.binom{5}{2} = frac{5!}{2!(5-2)!} = frac{5!}{2!3!} = frac{5 times 4}{2 times 1} = 10.(2x)^3 = 2^3 x^3 = 8x^3.(-frac{1}{sqrt{x}})^2 = (-frac{1}{x^{1/2}})^2 = (frac{1}{x^{1/2}})^2 = frac{1}{x} = x^{-1}.So, T_3 = binom{5}{2} (2x)^3 (-frac{1}{sqrt{x}})^2 = 10 cdot (8x^3) cdot (x^{-1}) = 10 cdot 8 cdot x^3 cdot x^{-1} = 80 x^{3-1} = 80 x^2.The coefficient of the x^2 term is 80.Let's double check the exponent of x when k=2.Exponent of x is 5-k - frac{k}{2} = 5-2 - frac{2}{2} = 5-2-1 = 2.So, when k=2, we have the x^2 term.The coefficient of the term is binom{5}{2} 2^{5-2} (-1)^2 = binom{5}{2} 2^3 (-1)^2 = 10 cdot 8 cdot 1 = 80.So the term is 80x^2.The coefficient of the x^2 term is 80.We can write out the terms of the expansion:k=0: binom{5}{0} (2x)^5 (-frac{1}{sqrt{x}})^0 = 1 cdot (32x^5) cdot 1 = 32x^5 exponent: 5-0-0/2 = 5k=1: binom{5}{1} (2x)^4 (-frac{1}{sqrt{x}})^1 = 5 cdot (16x^4) cdot (-frac{1}{sqrt{x}}) = -80 x^{4} x^{-1/2} = -80 x^{4-1/2} = -80 x^{7/2} exponent: 5-1-1/2 = 4-1/2 = 7/2k=2: binom{5}{2} (2x)^3 (-frac{1}{sqrt{x}})^2 = 10 cdot (8x^3) cdot (frac{1}{x}) = 80 x^{3-1} = 80 x^2 exponent: 5-2-2/2 = 3-1 = 2k=3: binom{5}{3} (2x)^2 (-frac{1}{sqrt{x}})^3 = 10 cdot (4x^2) cdot (-frac{1}{x^{3/2}}) = -40 x^{2} x^{-3/2} = -40 x^{2-3/2} = -40 x^{1/2} exponent: 5-3-3/2 = 2-3/2 = 1/2k=4: binom{5}{4} (2x)^1 (-frac{1}{sqrt{x}})^4 = 5 cdot (2x) cdot (frac{1}{x^2}) = 10 x x^{-2} = 10 x^{-1} exponent: 5-4-4/2 = 1-2 = -1k=5: binom{5}{5} (2x)^0 (-frac{1}{sqrt{x}})^5 = 1 cdot 1 cdot (-frac{1}{x^{5/2}}) = -x^{-5/2} exponent: 5-5-5/2 = -5/2The term with x^2 is obtained when k=2, and the term is 80x^2.The coefficient of the x^2 term is 80.
