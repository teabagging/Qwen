Each pack of DVDs costs 107 dollars. If there is a discount of 106 dollars on each pack, how many packs of DVDs can you buy with 93 dollars?

Let the cost of each pack of DVDs be C dollars.Given C = 107 dollars.Let the discount on each pack be D dollars.Given D = 106 dollars.Let the amount of money you have be M dollars.Given M = 93 dollars.We are asked to find out how many packs of DVDs we can buy with 93 dollars after the discount.First, we need to calculate the price of each pack of DVDs after the discount.The price of each pack of DVDs after discount = Original price - DiscountPrice after discount = C - D = 107 - 106 = 1 dollar.So, each pack of DVDs costs 1 dollar after the discount.We have 93 dollars to buy the packs of DVDs.Let the number of packs of DVDs we can buy be N.The total cost to buy N packs of DVDs after discount = N * (Price after discount)Total cost = N * 1 = N dollars.We want to find the maximum number of packs of DVDs we can buy with 93 dollars.So, the total cost should be less than or equal to the amount of money we have.N <= MN <= 93Since the price of each pack after discount is 1 dollar, and we have 93 dollars, we can buy 93 packs of DVDs.Number of packs of DVDs = Total money / Price of each pack after discountNumber of packs of DVDs = 93 / 1 = 93.So, you can buy 93 packs of DVDs with 93 dollars after the discount.Let's double check the steps.Cost of each pack = 107 dollars.Discount on each pack = 106 dollars.Price after discount = 107 - 106 = 1 dollar.Money available = 93 dollars.Number of packs you can buy = Money available / Price after discount = 93 / 1 = 93 packs.The answer seems correct.
